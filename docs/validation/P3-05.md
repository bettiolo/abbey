# P3-05 — Light-band combat and two-tier settler home defense

## What changed

Combat now resolves on the light-band gradient, and settlers defend their homes in two
tiers (sleep, then fire from lit windows) with destructible, razeable houses.

- **Resolver.** New `Runtime/Combat/LightBandCombatResolver.cs` — pure, deterministic,
  no RNG. `Resolve(side, isBeast, band, config)` returns a `CombatTreatment`
  (damage multiplier + sanity-drain rate): **Safe** debuffs monsters, **Edge** is even,
  **Dark** debuffs friendlies and drains their sanity, and the **beast is exempt in
  every band**. `ResolveAt(...)` classifies a world position through
  `DarknessEvaluator` (which already sees the P3-01 weather light-effectiveness
  multiplier). Every fighter routes damage through this one function.
- **Home defense.** New `Runtime/Combat/HomeDefenseSystem.cs` (singleton, `[ExecuteAlways]`,
  manual `Tick(dt)` in tests). Each night, per occupied destructible home:
  *Sleeping* → nothing happens, no sanity cost, while no monster is within
  `wakeRadius`; a monster at the door **wakes** the house (`HomeWokeForDefense`), flares
  its interior `LightSource` (a small Safe zone at the door), and occupants fire volleys
  on `windowShotIntervalSeconds` — damage through the resolver, `sanityCostPerVolley`
  charged to each occupant via `SanitySystem.ApplySanityCost`; if the home's hit points
  reach 0 it **razes**.
- **Destructible homes.** `Building` (modified) gains hit points, an occupant list, an
  interior-light flare (`FlareOn`/`FlareOff`), `TakeStructuralDamage` and `Raze`
  (kills occupants, destroys the light node so the doorstep reclassifies Dark, fires
  `SettlersKilledInHome` + `HomeRazed`). `BuildingType` gains `destructibleHome` (set on
  `shelter_t1`) and `homeHitPointMultiplier`.
- **Monster door-assault.** `MonsterController` (modified) now, when no *reachable*
  villager is exposed (a villager only in Safe light yields), charges the nearest
  occupied home through its door light and batters it — structural damage scaled by the
  resolver (Safe doorstep ⇒ debuffed). Legacy stalking is preserved when no occupied
  home exists.
- **Config.** All balance lives in the **`CombatConfig`** ScriptableObject (Resources
  path `CombatConfig`, `CreateInstance` defaults — no asset file required): band
  multipliers (`safe/edge/darkMonsterDamageMultiplier`,
  `safe/edge/darkFriendlyDamageMultiplier`), `darkFriendlySanityDrainPerSecond`, the
  home-defense set (`wakeRadius`, `flareLightRadius/Strength`, `windowShotDamage`,
  `windowShotIntervalSeconds`, `sanityCostPerVolley`, `defenseEngageRange`) and the raze
  set (`baseHomeHitPoints`, `homeHitPointMultiplier` per type via `HomeHitPointsFor`,
  `monsterHomeAttackDamage`).
- **Debug surface.** New **F6 `CombatDebugPanel`** (right side): band multipliers, live
  monster count, and every destructible home's state (Sleeping/Awake/Razed), band under
  the door, occupant count and a hit-point bar. `SettlerHomeBootstrap` assigns nearby
  settlers to the two prototype shelters via the SanitySystem shelter map on play-start.

Public API downstream tasks consume: `LightBandCombatResolver.Resolve` /
`ResolveAt` + `CombatTreatment` (P3-06 warriors fight in Dark through it; P3-08 Candle
Line shifts the bands the resolver reads), the `CombatConfig` fields, the beast-exempt
flag and `combat`/`window_shot`/`home_*` log records (P3-07 hound evolution reads combat
treatment history), and `HomeDefenseSystem.StateOf` / `EventBus.HomeWokeForDefense` /
`HomeRazed` / `SettlersKilledInHome`.

## Technical verification

Run in the local macOS Unity editor with MCP for Unity (the compile/test authority; CI
never compiles Unity — see `docs/VERIFICATION_STATUS.md`):

1. Start / connect the pinned editor + MCP bridge:
   - `tools/restart_unity_mcp.sh` (fresh), or
   - `UNITY_MCP_UV_OFFLINE=1 tools/run_unity_mcp_gate.sh --no-restart` when already
     connected.
2. Full gate: `tools/run_unity_mcp_gate.sh` (Unity gate + EditMode + PlayMode + console
   check). Everything must be green with **no console errors**.

New test fixtures for this task:

- EditMode: `Abbey.Tests.EditMode.LightBandCombatTests` — **9 tests** (monster debuffed
  in Safe / even in Edge and Dark; friendly even in Safe and Edge, debuffed + sanity
  drain in Dark; beast exempt in every band for both sides; multipliers come from the
  injected config not constants; `ResolveAt` classifies through `DarknessEvaluator`;
  home hit points scale by the building-type multiplier; a flare makes the doorstep Safe;
  razing kills occupants, removes the light node and fires the events; structural damage
  is ignored on a non-home).
- PlayMode: `Abbey.Tests.PlayMode.HomeDefensePlayModeTests` — **4 tests** (danger far
  away ⇒ the house sleeps all night at no sanity cost; a monster at the door ⇒ wake
  event, flare makes the doorstep Safe, volleys damage the monster and occupants lose
  sanity per volley; a Safe flare debuffs the monster's home damage by exactly
  `monsterHomeAttackDamage × safeMonsterDamageMultiplier`; an overwhelmed fragile home
  is razed — occupants dead, light node gone/Dark, `HomeRazed` on the event stream).

Expected totals after this task: EditMode **215 + 9 = 224**, PlayMode **48 + 4 = 52**
(baseline after P3-04 was 215 / 48). A different count means a regression or a merge
added/removed tests — investigate before trusting the gate.

Container check (no Unity): `./tools/check_all.sh` — must pass. Every Unity step SKIPs
(normal in the container). The `REQUIREMENTS.yml` tracker test must stay green with P3-05
at `done` and `validated_commit: a4e99a6`. No new assets are introduced, so the Blender
asset gate is unaffected.

## Visual verification

1. `Tools → Abbey → Build Prototype Scene`, then press Play. The camp now places two
   **destructible-home shelters** (`Shelter_A`, `Shelter_B`); `SettlerHomeBootstrap`
   houses the nearest settlers in them via the SanitySystem shelter map.
2. Press **F6** (`CombatDebugPanel`). You should see the band multipliers (monster
   Safe < 1, friendly Dark < 1, beast exempt), the live monster count, and each shelter
   listed **[Sleeping]** with `band=Safe/Edge`, an occupant count and a full hit-point
   bar. Open **F9** (`SanityDebugPanel`) alongside to watch occupant sanity.
3. Force a night and spawn a monster near a shelter (advance to Night, then place/aim a
   nightmare at a shelter door — `NightmareDebug`/`NightmareDirector` or scene placement).
   **Correct (lit-window defense):**
   - the shelter row flips to **[Awake]** and its **interior flare light gizmo pops on**
     (the doorstep now reads `band=Safe`);
   - the monster's HP falls under window fire, and in F9 the occupants' **sanity ticks
     down** each volley (a `window_shot` line appears in the log tail);
   - a single monster is driven off / killed and the home survives (hit-point bar dips
     but holds).
   **Correct (razed home):** let a second (or hard-hitting) monster join. The monsters
   brave the Safe door light (their strikes are debuffed but they persist), the
   hit-point bar drains to 0, and the home **collapses**: the flare **gizmo light
   disappears** (doorstep back to `band=Dark`), the F6 row reads **[Razed]** with
   occupants gone, and the **morning report mentions the loss** (`HomeRazed` /
   `SettlersKilledInHome` on the event stream).
   **Failure:** the house wakes with danger far away, occupants lose sanity while asleep,
   the flare never makes the door Safe, a razed home keeps its light gizmo, or the beast
   (hound) is debuffed by a band.
4. Cross-check balance against data, not code: open the `CombatConfig` asset (or the
   coded defaults in `Runtime/Combat/CombatConfig.cs`) and confirm the band multipliers,
   `wakeRadius`, `flareLightRadius`, `windowShotDamage`/`Interval`, `sanityCostPerVolley`,
   `baseHomeHitPoints` and `monsterHomeAttackDamage` match what the F6 panel shows.
