# P3-12 ‚Äî Desire paths and ground scars

## What changed

Two ground-memory systems sharing one world-space grid, both tuned entirely from the
**`PathsConfig`** ScriptableObject (Resources path `PathsConfig`, coded defaults ‚Äî no
asset required).

**Desire paths (persistent infrastructure).** A `TrafficGrid` (on `WorldSystems`)
accumulates distance-proportional *wear* per cell from villager foot traffic. Every
path-wearing walker reports its travelled segment automatically: `PlanarMotion.StepWorn`
(now used by `VillagerAgent` and `VillagerJobAgent` movement) is wired to the grid's
`ReportTraversal`, so villagers wear roads with no per-agent code, while monsters/hound ‚Äî
which call the plain `PlanarMotion.Step` ‚Äî never wear paths. `DesirePathSystem` turns wear
into *tiers*: higher tiers grant a walk-speed bonus (also applied through
`PlanarMotion.StepWorn` via `PathSpeedProvider`), untrodden paths decay each day
(`EventBus.DayChanged`), and at **Dusk** it scans *important* paths (tier ‚â•
`importantTier`): a lantern covering one is set to burn fuel at `importantPathFuelMultiplier`
through the night (fuel debt, via the new `LightSource.PathFuelMultiplier`), while an unlit
important-path cell adds to the settlement light debt (folded into
`SeedSlotSystem.ComputeLightDebt`, P3-02). Dawn clears the fuel multipliers. Child seed
slots now also hug existing desire paths.

**Ground scars (transient violence).** `GroundScarSystem` buffers the night's violence by
position (P3-05 `EventBus.HomeRazed` / `SettlersKilledInHome`, which carry the home
GameObject) and stamps them as dark scars at **Dawn**. Scars fade to meadow across the day
and are gone before **Dusk** ‚Äî except in **Winter** (P3-01), where they persist
`snowCovered = true` instead of regrowing. Nearby stamps merge.

Debug surface: the existing **SettlementDebugPanel (key `F10`)** gained a desire-path
section (path/important cell counts, lit-path light debt, fuel-boosted lantern count) and a
ground-scar section (scar count, snow-covered count, season); press **`K`** to toggle the
traffic-wear **heatmap** gizmo.

Added: `Runtime/Settlement/PathsConfig.cs`, `Runtime/Settlement/TrafficGrid.cs`,
`Runtime/Settlement/DesirePathSystem.cs`, `Runtime/Settlement/GroundScarSystem.cs`,
`Tests/EditMode/DesirePathTests.cs`, `Tests/EditMode/GroundScarDecayTests.cs`,
`Tests/PlayMode/DesirePathPlayModeTests.cs`. Modified: `Runtime/Core/PlanarMotion.cs`
(traffic hooks + `StepWorn`), `Runtime/Light/LightSource.cs` (`PathFuelMultiplier`),
`Runtime/Villagers/VillagerAgent.cs` + `VillagerJobAgent.cs` (route movement through
`StepWorn`), `Runtime/Settlement/SeedSlotSystem.cs` (path light debt + path-hug),
`Runtime/Debug/SettlementDebugPanel.cs` (readouts + heatmap toggle),
`Editor/PrototypeSceneBuilder.cs` (spawns the three systems; grid sized to map bounds).

- `Abbey.Tests.EditMode.DesirePathTests` ‚Äî **8 tests**: repeated traversals promote a cell
  from tier 0 to tier 1 to an important tier; unused paths decay each day (and heal to zero

  a walker exactly the tier's multiplier further on a worn cell; a lantern over an important

- `Abbey.Tests.PlayMode.DesirePathPlayModeTests` ‚Äî **1 test**: a salvager runs its
  site ‚Üí storage loop for several unattended fast days and the grid shows a desire path

## Visual verification

1. `Tools ‚Üí Abbey ‚Üí Build Prototype Scene`, then press Play. `WorldSystems` now also holds a
   `TrafficGrid` (sized to the ¬±48 map bounds), a `DesirePathSystem` and a
   `GroundScarSystem`.
2. Press **`F10`** for the **Settlement** panel (bottom-right); it now lists desire-path
   cell/important counts, lit-path light debt and fuel-boosted lanterns, plus the ground-scar
   count/snow-covered/season line. Press **`K`** to toggle the wear **heatmap**.
3. **Watch roads emerge.** Let villagers work for 2‚Äì3 days. **Correct:** warm heat trails
   appear *exactly* along real villager commutes (worksite ‚Üî storage ‚Üî camp) and sharpen
   from faint green into amber/red path tiers; the `F10` path-cell count climbs. Ground the
   villagers never cross stays clean. Leaving a route unused across a few dawns cools it
   (daily decay).
4. **Feel the trade-off.** Once a commute reaches an *important* tier, at dusk a lantern
   sitting on it starts draining fuel faster (its `LightSource.PathFuelMultiplier` reads
   `importantPathFuelMultiplier`, logged as `desire_path dusk ‚Ä¶`); if that stretch is left
   dark, the settlement light debt on `F10` rises (unlit-important cells feed it). At dawn
   the fuel rate returns to normal.
5. **See the night's scars.** After a violent night (a razed settler home ‚Äî force one via
   the combat/home-defense flow), at **Dawn** dark scar decals appear where the home fell
   (`ground_scar stamp ‚Ä¶` in the log). Across the day they fade and are **gone by Dusk**.
6. **Flip to Winter.** Open the **Season** panel (**`F11`**) and advance to Winter (or run
   days until Winter). Trigger violence again: the dawn scars now stay all day, shown as
   pale **snow-covered** patches (heatmap/scar gizmo turns whitish; `snow=True` in the log
   and the `F10` snow-covered count is non-zero). "Broken" would be scars that regrow in
   Winter, never appear at dawn, or appear where no fight happened.
7. Cross-check balance against data, not code: open `Runtime/Settlement/PathsConfig.cs` (or a
   `Resources/PathsConfig` asset) and confirm the wear/decay rates, tier thresholds, speed
   multipliers, `importantPathFuelMultiplier`, light-debt-per-cell and scar fade/merge/winter
   values match what the `F10` panel and the `desire_path` / `ground_scar` log records show.
