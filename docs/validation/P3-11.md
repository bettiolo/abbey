# P3-11 — Consequence nightmares and threat-source pressure

## What changed

Two spawn-shaping systems that make the night reflect the settlement's choices.

**WHAT spawns** — five *consequence nightmares* (`ConsequenceNightmareCatalog`), each armed by
moral state rather than the fixed night script: **Hunger Wights** (Fasting food law OR high
Hunger pressure), **Dead Workers** (Forced night-labour law AND a logged villager death),
**Grave Crawlers** (the Mass Graves / Use the Dead per-death grave tags P3-09 stamps),
**Chain Hounds** (Chained hound doctrine OR a Broken abbey), **Faceless Saints** (pagan rites
forbidden AND Old-faith high OR Sanctity collapsed). All five share one
`ConsequenceMonsterController` (base hunt behaviour, per-species health/bell-stun from config).

**WHERE it spawns** — a `ThreatSourceSystem` registers the seven exploitable places (forest,
well, cave, mountain, shore, crypt, old road) and folds *exploitation pressure* from the event
log exactly like `PressureSystem` (woodcutting→forest, coal→cave, stone→mountain,
salvage→shore, grave handling→crypt, the daily draw→well, hauling→old road); pressure decays at
each day marker and drops when a source is mitigated (`Mitigate`, logged as `threat_mitigation`).
The `NightmareDirector` now places both the base wave and the consequence nightmares with
`SelectWeightedSource` — a seeded draw biased toward the most-pressured sources (plus a bonus for
a rule's preferred source).

All balance lives in **`ThreatConfig`** (Resources path `ThreatConfig`, coded defaults — no asset
required): the five trigger rules (tags, pressure thresholds, spawn counts, preferred source,
per-type stats), the event→source exploitation map, decay / clamp / day-marker and the
spawn-weight floor + preferred bonus. Deterministic (seeded spawns; pure log fold).

Added: `Runtime/Nightmares/ThreatConfig.cs`, `Runtime/Nightmares/ConsequenceNightmareCatalog.cs`,
`Runtime/Nightmares/ConsequenceMonsterController.cs`, `Runtime/Nightmares/ThreatSourceSystem.cs`,
`Tests/EditMode/ConsequenceNightmareTests.cs`, `Tests/EditMode/ThreatSourceTests.cs`,
`Tests/PlayMode/ConsequenceNightmarePlayModeTests.cs`, the four monster specs + generated
artifacts. Modified: `NightmareSchedule.cs` (5 new `NightmareType`s), `NightmareDirector.cs`
(source-weighted + consequence spawns), `MonsterController.cs` (`HealthScale` hook),
`Debug/NightmareDebugPanel.cs` (threat + armed-trigger sections), `Editor/PrototypeSceneBuilder.cs`
(spawns `ThreatSourceSystem` + registers the seven source locations). Debug surface: the existing
**NightmareDebugPanel (key `F4`)** now also shows the per-source pressure bars and tonight's armed
consequence nightmares.

2. Full gate: `tools/run_unity_mcp_gate.sh` (Unity gate + EditMode + PlayMode + console check).

New test fixtures for this task (programmatic worlds, injected `ThreatConfig`):

- `Abbey.Tests.EditMode.ConsequenceNightmareTests` — **9 tests**: a neutral state arms nothing;
  fasting arms Hunger Wights but NOT Grave Crawlers/Dead Workers; high Hunger arms Hunger Wights
  with no tag; Forced labour arms Dead Workers only once a death is logged; a lone death without
  the law does not; the mass-grave / use-the-dead tags arm Grave Crawlers (an honoured grave does
  not); Chained doctrine OR a Broken abbey arm Chain Hounds; forbidding rites alone does nothing
  but forbidding-rites + high Old-faith / low Sanctity arms the Faceless Saints.

  an untouched source stays zero; salvage/graves map to shore/crypt; day markers decay pressure;
  mitigation lowers it; the same log folds twice identically; the seeded weighted draw shifts
  toward the high-pressure source and is seed-reproducible; a preferred source gets its bonus.

  `grave_mass` tag in the log spawns a Grave Crawler beside the pressured crypt source; the same
  seed with no grave tag spawns none.

## Visual verification

1. `Tools → Abbey → Build Prototype Scene`, then press Play. `WorldSystems` now also holds a
   `ThreatSourceSystem` with seven registered source locations (forest edge, beach, abbey hill
   crypt, camp well, the camp→forest road, a hillside cave, the far mountain).
2. Press **F4** to open the **Nightmare** panel (bottom-right). Below the schedule/monster
   sections it now shows **Threat sources** (one exploitation-pressure bar per source type) and
   **Armed consequence nightmares (tonight)** — the species currently armed, their spawn count and
   preferred source. With the humane default laws and a fresh log, nothing is armed.
3. **Chop wood for days → the forest bar climbs.** Let villagers work the woodcutter post and pass
   several dawns. **Correct:** the `Forest` bar on F4 grows as `resource wood +N` records fold in,
   and cools slightly across days with no cutting (decay); salvage grows `Shore`, the daily ration
   pass nudges `Well`, burials grow `Crypt`.
4. **Arm a consequence nightmare via the laws.** Open the **Laws** panel (**L**) and **Shift+1**
   until Food shows **Fasting**; let a dawn pass so Hunger climbs (watch the **M** pressures
   panel). **Correct:** F4's "Armed consequence nightmares" lists **HungerWight @ Well**; on the
   next Phase-3 night the director logs `consequence_nightmare armed type=HungerWight` and a Hunger
   Wight spawns out by the pressured well (compare its spawn position against the well source).
   Similarly: **Shift+3 → Mass Graves** and let a villager die → **GraveCrawler @ Crypt** rising by
   the abbey-hill graves; **Shift+2 → Forced** night labour with a death → **DeadWorker @ Mountain**;
   **Shift+4 → Chained** hound → **ChainHound @ OldRoad**; forbid rites while sanctity is low →
   **FacelessSaint @ Shore**.
5. **Placement follows pressure.** A species spawns near its preferred source when that source is
   pressured; a wholly un-exploited map falls back to the dark ring. Resting a source
   (`ThreatSourceSystem.Mitigate`) drops its bar and its pull on placement.
6. **The four new monster previews** read as distinct silhouettes at iso distance — inspect
   `blender/generated/previews/{hunger_wight,grave_crawler,chain_hound,faceless_saint}_lowpoly_preview_{day,night,winter,grayscale}.png`:
   the **Hunger Wight** a tall gaunt reaching figure with a distended belly; the **Grave Crawler**
   a low, long, many-limbed thing dragging along the ground; the **Chain Hound** the black
   hound's heavy build with a massive iron collar and a dragging chain + ember eyes; the
   **Faceless Saint** a tall pale robed column with a blank golden face and a thin gold halo.
7. Cross-check balance against data, not code: open the coded defaults in
   `Runtime/Nightmares/ThreatConfig.cs` (or a `Resources/ThreatConfig` asset) and confirm the
   trigger thresholds, exploitation rates, decay and spawn weights match what the F4 panel and the
   `consequence_nightmare` / `threat_mitigation` log records show.
