# P3-03 — Sanity, dread and the asylum

## What changed

A new layer under `Runtime/Sanity/` adds the game's **persistent damage track**
(health is the transient one). `SanitySystem` (a singleton like `SeasonSystem`) keeps
a `SanityRecord` per `VillagerAgent` — found through the `DuskRecallSystem` registry,
so the hound (not a villager) is never tracked and stays immune — and runs the
dark-exposure pipeline: in the **Dark at Night** dread rises; sustained dread past the
config threshold eats into sanity; sanity below the insanity threshold flips the
villager **Insane** (raising `EventBus.VillagerWentInsane`). **Morning (Dawn)** resets
health (Injured/Resting villagers return to Idle) but sanity persists. **Day** admits
an insane villager to the `AsylumZone` (parked, missing the coming night, recovering
fast) and releases it only by day once the cooldown has elapsed and sanity has
recovered; with **no asylum** the insane settler recovers slowly at home and each night
spreads dread to its housemates (the spill). Daytime **work efficiency** scales with
sanity (insane ⇒ zero: villagers down tools). All thresholds/rates live in the
`SanityConfig` ScriptableObject (Resources path `SanityConfig`, `CreateInstance`
defaults like `EconomyConfig` — no asset file required). Hidden state is shown on the
new **F9 SanityDebugPanel** (per-villager sanity + dread bars, asylum roster, held
count).

`AsylumZone` (the P3-02 shell) gains the admission roster + cooldown + parking; the
per-second sanity-recovery rate itself is applied by `SanitySystem` (it owns the value
and the config). `VillagerAgent` gains `SanityWorkEfficiency`; `VillagerJobAgent`
scales its work-cycle yield by it and downs tools at 0.

Public API downstream tasks consume: `SanitySystem.Instance`
(`Records`, `RecordFor`, `TryGetRecord`, `IsHeldInAsylum`, `IsAvailableForNight`,
`AverageSanity`, `AssignHome`/`HomeOf`, `ApplySanityCost`, `AddDread`), the
`SanityRecord`/`SanityState` types, and the `EventBus` sanity events. (P3-05 lit-window
home defense charges sanity via `ApplySanityCost` and reuses the home map; P3-08
overdrive adds nightmare debt via `AddDread`; P3-10 aggregates household sanity via
`AverageSanity`/`Records`.)

Run in the local macOS Unity editor with MCP for Unity (the compile/test authority;
CI never compiles Unity — see `docs/VERIFICATION_STATUS.md`):

1. Start / connect the pinned editor + MCP bridge:
   - `tools/restart_unity_mcp.sh` (fresh), or
   - `UNITY_MCP_UV_OFFLINE=1 tools/run_unity_mcp_gate.sh --no-restart` when already
     connected.
2. Full gate: `tools/run_unity_mcp_gate.sh` (Unity gate + EditMode + PlayMode +
   console check). Everything must be green with **no console errors**.

New test fixtures for this task:

- EditMode: `Abbey.Tests.EditMode.SanitySystemTests` — **6 tests** (dread accumulates
  only in the Dark at night; sustained dark crosses the threshold, flips Insane and
  logs `VillagerWentInsane`; the work-efficiency multiplier follows the config curve;
  reduced sanity scales a woodcutter's yield and insanity stops work entirely; morning
  resets a wounded villager's health but not its sanity; the beast is never tracked).
- PlayMode: `Abbey.Tests.PlayMode.SanityAsylumPlayModeTests` — **3 tests** (insane
  villager admitted to the asylum misses the following night — absent from
  recall/defense participation — and is released only by day after the cooldown;
  without an asylum, home recovery is slower and each housemate gains dread per night;
  the hound never appears in sanity records).

Expected totals after this task: EditMode **200 + 6 = 206**, PlayMode **43 + 3 = 46**
(baseline after P3-02 was 200 / 43). A different count means a regression or a merge
added/removed tests — investigate before trusting the gate.

Container check (no Unity): `./tools/check_all.sh` — design validation and the
requirements tracker test must PASS; every Unity step SKIPs (normal here). The
`REQUIREMENTS.yml` tracker test must stay green with P3-03 at `in_review`.

## Visual verification

1. `Tools → Abbey → Build Prototype Scene`, then press Play.
2. Press **F9** to open the SanityDebugPanel (top-left). It lists each tracked
   villager's band, a `san` bar and a `dread` bar, the average sanity, the asylum
   admitted count and how many are held tonight.
   - **Correct:** by day every villager reads `Stable` with a full `san` bar and an
     empty `dread` bar.
   - **Failure:** "no SanitySystem in scene", or bars that never move.
3. Force a villager into the dark at night: let dusk fall (or move a villager out
   past the lantern-lit path with the scene view), and do **not** recall it.
   - **Correct:** in the Dark at Night its `dread` bar climbs; once dread passes the
     threshold its `san` bar starts dropping and the band steps
     `Stable → Shaken → Breaking → Insane`. The event log shows `sanity_state ->Insane`
     and a `VillagerWentInsane` record.
   - **Failure:** dread rises in Safe/Edge light or by day, or sanity never falls.
4. Asylum admission (build the abbey **Asylum Corner** first, F3 / abbey hill):
   - **Correct:** at the next dawn/day the insane villager walks to the asylum corner
     and is parked there; the F9 panel shows it `[asylum]` in the roster and `held 1`;
     through the following night it does nothing (no jobs, no recall), and on a later
     **day** — after the cooldown — it is discharged (`AsylumReleased`) and returns to
     Idle with a recovered `san` bar.
   - **Failure:** an insane villager keeps working, is released at night, or is never
     admitted.
5. No-asylum household spill (raze/omit the asylum): keep an insane settler at home.
   - **Correct:** each night the F9 `dread` bars of its housemates tick up (a
     `HouseholdDisturbed` log record), and the insane settler's `san` bar creeps back
     up **slowly** by day (slower than the asylum would restore it).
   - **Failure:** housemates gain no dread, or the home villager recovers as fast as
     in the asylum.
6. Sanity vs the config: open the `SanityConfig` asset (or read the coded defaults in
   `Runtime/Sanity/SanityConfig.cs`) to confirm the panel behaviour matches the rates
   (`dreadGainPerSecondInDark`, `dreadDamageThreshold`, `insanityThreshold`,
   `asylumCooldownDays`, `asylumRecoveryPerSecond` vs `homeRecoveryPerSecond`,
   `dreadSpillPerNight`, the work-efficiency floor/full-sanity curve).
