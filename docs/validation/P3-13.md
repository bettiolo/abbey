# P3-13 — Island exploration, arrivals and dilemmas

## What changed

Three event-driven island systems on one seeded framework, all tuned from a single
**`IslandConfig`** ScriptableObject (Resources path `IslandConfig`, coded defaults — no asset
required), living under `Runtime/Island/`:

- **Exploration** (`ExplorationSystem` + `PointOfInterest`). Authored hidden points of
  interest (wreckage, old road, shrine, well, boundary stone, survivor camp, resource cache)
  sit out in the fog. The player sends a party of villagers to one; the party walks out (foot
  traffic wears desire paths to the POI via `PlanarMotion.StepWorn`, P3-12), surveys it — which
  reveals it and lands its data-driven reward: resources to the `ResourceLedger`, new **open
  seed slots** through `SeedSlotSystem.UnlockSlotsNear` (P3-02), people through the arrival
  channel, and shrine/well **threat sources** into `ThreatSourceSystem` (P3-11) — then walks
  home. Party villagers are pulled off their jobs while away (`JobAssigner` skips them); an
  expedition not home by **Dusk** is caught out in the dark (handed to the normal dusk recall).
- **Arrivals** (`ArrivalSystem`). Population growth through three channels: passive dawn
  walk-ins (a deterministic per-day seed draw), people an expedition finds, and storm
  **shipwreck** crews thrown ashore with supplies. Each newcomer is integrated by the P3-10
  **trust tier**: below the stay tier they refuse and record a spring **departure intent**
  (for the P3-14 end summary); at/above it they stay; at the volunteer tier they also
  volunteer. A shipwreck rescue arms the director's **drowned-nightmare window** for N nights.
  Newcomers who stay are instantiated as villagers with a class tag (specialist = a job-speed
  bonus, warrior = a P3-06 recruitment candidate).
- **Dilemmas** (`DilemmaSystem`). A FIFO queue of data-driven choice cards. The three named
  cards ship in the config deck: **Missing Salvager** (search at night / write off),
  **Food Thief** (punish / forgive / exile), **Hound Bites a Child** (punish hound / protect
  hound / compensate family). Each option applies exactly its configured effects: signed
  moral-pressure deltas (folded by `PressureSystem` via a new `pressure_delta` record),
  law-like tags, resource compensations, and hound treatment/doctrine inputs to P3-07.

Debug surface: the new **`IslandDebugPanel`** (key **`I`**, bottom-left) lists POIs +
discovered state, live expeditions, the arrival tier/history + spring departures, and the
pending dilemma. While open: number keys **1-3** resolve the dilemma, **O** launches an
expedition to the nearest hidden POI, **U** triggers a storm shipwreck, **Y** raises the next
dilemma card.

New test fixtures for this task (programmatic worlds, injected `IslandConfig`):

- `Abbey.Tests.EditMode.ExplorationTests` — **5 tests**: an expedition reveals its POI and
  applies the ledger + seed-slot reward; the party is pulled off jobs while away (roster

## Visual verification

1. `Tools → Abbey → Build Prototype Scene`, then press Play. `WorldSystems` now also holds an
   `ExplorationSystem`, an `ArrivalSystem` and a `DilemmaSystem`; hidden POIs ring the map edge.
2. Press **`I`** for the **Island** panel (bottom-left). It lists the POIs (all `[ ]` hidden at
   first), expeditions (0), the arrival trust tier + stayed/volunteer/leaving counts, and the
   pending dilemma (none).
3. **Run an expedition.** With the panel open press **`O`**: it musters up to three idle
   villagers and sends them to the nearest hidden POI (e.g. the far-shore wreckage). **Correct:**
   the party walks *off toward the map edge during the day* (a faint desire path forms behind
   them), the POI flips to `[x]` discovered on the panel with its reward logged
   (`poi_discovered …`, `resource … (expedition …)`, `slot_unlocked …`), then the party walks
   back into camp. "Broken" = the party teleports, the POI never flips, or the reward is missing.
4. **See an arrival.** Passive survivors walk in on scheduled dawns; or press **`U`** to force a
   storm shipwreck. **Correct:** supplies land in the ledger and the crew resolves by trust —
   with high trust the panel's *stayed / volunteers* counts rise and new `Newcomer_*` villagers
   appear near camp; with low trust the *leaving spring* count rises instead (they linger and
   record a departure). A wet rescue logs `drowned_risk_armed …`, and for the next few nights a
   drowned sailor may rise by the wreck.
5. **Resolve a dilemma.** Press **`Y`** to raise a card (cycles the deck to Hound Bites a Child).
   The panel shows the card id and its options `1./2./3.`. Press **`1`/`2`/`3`** to choose.
   **Correct:** the chosen option's consequences land — open the **pressure panel (`M`)** and see
   the deltas (punish raises Fear / drops Mercy; compensate raises Mercy and debits food/medicine
   on the economy panel `F2`); Hound Bites a Child → punish writes `hound_treatment kind=punish`
   and sets the hound doctrine to Chained (visible on the pressure/hound overlays), protect costs
   trust, compensate spends supplies. The card leaves the queue after the choice.
6. Cross-check balance against data, not code: open `Runtime/Island/IslandConfig.cs` (or a
   `Resources/IslandConfig` asset) and confirm the expedition speed/resolve time, POI reward
   table, passive cadence + trust gates, shipwreck crew/supplies + drowned window, and the three
   dilemma decks match what the `I` panel and the `poi_*` / `arrival` / `dilemma_*` log records
   show.
