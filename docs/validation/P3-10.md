# P3-10 — Moral pressures, household sanity and abbey transformation

## What changed

A deterministic **moral-pressure store** and the **abbey transformation** it drives. A new
**`PressureSystem`** (namespace `Abbey.Morale`, on `WorldSystems`, singleton,
`[ExecuteAlways]`) folds the append-only `GameEventLog` — deaths, razed homes, rescues, the
per-death grave tags + old-rite records the P3-09 laws write, overdrive `trust_pressure`
records — plus the active law tags into seven scalar pressures: **Trust, Sanctity, Mercy,
Fear, Reason, Hunger, Old-faith**. The fold is a *pure recompute* (`RecomputeFromLog`): it
resets every channel to its config baseline and re-applies the whole log in order, so the
**same log always yields the same pressures**; decay toward baseline is interleaved at each
day-marker record (`RationsIssued`), so decay is folded too, not wall-clock. Household sanity
(P3-03) and beast status (P3-07) are read live as transformation inputs. Trust exposes tier
bands (`TrustTier`) that gate volunteer behaviours (`IsVolunteerEligible`).

A new **`AbbeyTransformationSystem`** (same namespace / object) evaluates the abbey's form
each dawn from a `PressureSnapshot`: it scores **Sanctuary / Fortress / Famine / Cult /
Broken** and adopts the highest that clears its activation threshold — with **hysteresis** so
a marginal one-day swing does not flip the identity — or stays **Balanced**. The adopted form
pushes data-driven modifiers onto **`AbbeyState`** (`AbbeyState.CurrentForm` +
`AbbeyState.Modifiers`: sacred-light radius, window volley, ration ceiling, recall penalty,
offerings / sanctity decay). Transitions are event-logged (`abbey_transformation`).

All balance lives in **`PressuresConfig`** (Resources path `PressuresConfig`, coded defaults —
no asset required): channel baselines/decay/clamps, the event→delta weight table, the day
marker, trust-tier thresholds, volunteer gates, and the per-form scoring weights + activation
thresholds + modifiers. Deterministic (no RNG).

`SanitySystem` gained the household aggregates `HouseholdSanity` / `HouseholdMinSanity` /
`AverageHouseholdSanity`. The P3-08 `TrustLedger` / `BeastStatusLedger` write-side stubs were
**kept** (they still record overdrive costs into the log); `PressureSystem` is now the single
authoritative *derived* store and folds those `trust_pressure` records — so no P3-08 code was
disturbed and Trust is a pure function of the log.

Added: `Runtime/Morale/PressureDefinitions.cs`, `Runtime/Morale/PressuresConfig.cs`,
`Runtime/Morale/PressureSystem.cs`, `Runtime/Morale/AbbeyTransformationSystem.cs`,
`Runtime/Debug/PressureDebugPanel.cs`, `Tests/EditMode/PressureUpdateTests.cs`,
`Tests/EditMode/AbbeyTransformationTests.cs`. Modified: `Runtime/Buildings/AbbeyState.cs`
(transformation form + modifiers), `Runtime/Sanity/SanitySystem.cs` (household aggregates),
`Editor/PrototypeSceneBuilder.cs` (spawns the two systems + panel). Debug surface:
**`PressureDebugPanel`** (key **M** — the F1-F12 row is full and **L** is the Laws panel).

## Visual verification

1. `Tools → Abbey → Build Prototype Scene`, then press Play. `WorldSystems` now also holds a
   `PressureSystem` and an `AbbeyTransformationSystem`; `DebugPanels` holds a
   `PressureDebugPanel`.
2. Press **M** to open the **Pressures** panel (bottom-left). It lists all seven pressures
   with the Bellkeeper trust tier, beast status + household sanity, the current abbey form + a
   modifier line, and — the "why" — each candidate form's score against its activation
   threshold (a `*` marks a form that has cleared its bar), plus the active law tags.
3. **Rough night → fear jumps, trust dips.** Spawn monsters (nightmare panel F12/overdrive or
   let the night escalate) and let a home fall. At the next **dawn** the fold reruns:
   **correct** = Fear climbs and Trust drops on the M panel, and the `home_razed` /
   `villager_died` records that caused it are in the event log (the panel's per-form scores
   shift accordingly). **Broken** = pressures unchanged at dawn despite deaths/razes in the log.
4. **Drive the abbey into a state.** Open the **Laws** panel (**L**) and **Shift+1** until Food
   shows **Fasting**; let several dawns pass. **Correct:** Hunger climbs each day on the M
   panel, the **Famine** row's score crosses its threshold (`*`), the form banner flips to
   **Famine**, and its modifier line reads the ration-ceiling cut (`AbbeyState.Modifiers`).
   Similarly: stack Full Rites + honoured dead + high trust for **Sanctuary** (sacred light
   bonus); harsh fear + forced night labour for **Fortress** (window volley); Tolerate
   Offerings for **Cult** (offerings enabled); mass deaths + collapsed trust/household sanity
   for **Broken** (recall penalty).
5. **Broken vs the others.** Broken is the failure identity — it keys off *low* trust and *low*
   household sanity (mass insanity), not a positive stat, and its modifier is a recall-
   compliance **penalty**. The other four are driven by a *dominant* pressure and grant a
   capability or cost; Broken should only appear when the settlement is genuinely coming apart.
6. **Hysteresis.** Once a form is showing, a single day's marginal swing toward another form
   does **not** flip the banner (the challenger must beat the current score by the config
   margin); a decisive, sustained swing does. **Broken** behaviour would be the banner
   flip-flopping day to day.
7. Cross-check balance against data, not code: open `PressuresConfig` (or the coded defaults in
   `Runtime/Morale/PressuresConfig.cs`) and confirm the weight table, decay rates, trust-tier
   thresholds and per-form scoring weights / thresholds / modifiers match what the M panel and
   the `abbey_transformation` log records show.
